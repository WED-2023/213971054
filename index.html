About Me

Name: David Levy

Age: 71

Place of Birth: Jerusalem

I was born in 1950 in Jerusalem, a city where the streets are filled with the smells of fresh pastries and the sounds of children playing outside. Even as a child, I was drawn to reading and writing, spending hours in the city library imagining distant and fascinating worlds.

After completing my military service in the Artillery Corps, I pursued a career in education, eager to pass on my love of knowledge. For over 40 years, I worked as a history teacher in high school, where I taught generations of students about the importance of the past and its influence on the present.

  • Web2: Interactive content, user-generated platforms, centralized control by companies.
  • Web3: Decentralized applications (dApps), blockchain-based, user-controlled data.

Q2:answer

  • Web1: You would visit a static website with basic text-based information. Results may come from a medical encyclopedia or government health page. The content is not interactive and cannot be personalized.
  • Web2: You would search on a platform like Google or Wikipedia, and you would see dynamic, user-generated content like forums, articles, and reviews. The search results would be personalized based on your previous browsing habits and profile, but the data is controlled by centralized entities.
  • Web3: You would access a decentralized medical dApp that pulls information from a distributed network of verified contributors. The search results could include peer-reviewed medical documents, user testimonials, and verified medical data, where you, as a user, can control and verify your own data and possibly even contribute information in a secure, decentralized way.
